The identified AI proficiency needed for supporting educational practices

The figure presents the discussed required knowledge in the retrieved papers for each of the mentioned AI applications. From the figure, it can be found that researchers recognized the importance of ethical awareness (i.e., indicated by the size of the bar) while applying AI technologies in educational settings (Hays et al., 2024; Stolpe & Hallström, 2024; Zhang & Zhang, 2024; etc.). Moreover, other discussed capabilities that educators need to possess include the ability to harness AI tools (Wang et al., 2023; Ng et al., 2023; Lérias et al., 2024; etc.), prompt engineering knowledge (Kaplan-Rakowski et al., 2023; van den Berg & du Plessis, 2023; Black et al., 2024; etc.), and data literacy (Wilton et al., 2022; Chiu et al., 2024). For the pedagogical strategies, prior studies discussed that educators need to have the abilities such as maintaining a collaborative (with AI) learning environment (Meng et al., 2022; Adams et al., 2023), working as facilitators (Black et al., 2024), treating AI as collaborators (Koh et al., 2023; Kim, 2024) and assistants (Ji et al., 2024), maintaining pedagogical oversight (ElSayary, 2023), and balancing the human expertise and AI automation (Wang, 2023; Al-Mughairi & Bhaskar, 2024; Hashem et al., 2024; etc.).
