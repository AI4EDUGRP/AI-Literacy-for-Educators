Challenges

The common issues discussed by researchers include data privacy (Kim, 2024), data security (Adams et al., 2023), bias (Wang et al., 2023), transparency (Al-Mughairi & Bhaskar, 2024), justice (Adams et al., 2023) and so on. Additionally, researchers also expressed their concerns about the misuse of AI technologies and materials, such as when AI is not used responsibly and inclusively (Pretorius, 2023), when internet resources are misused (Vazhayil et al., 2019), or when inaccurate AI-generated results (Stolpe & Hallström, 2024) are used without thorough investigation, all of which could potentially give rise to additional issues. There are also studies highlighting concerns about the impact of overreliance on AI, including potential negative effects on students’ critical thinking abilities (Henry et al., 2021), human communication skills (Kaplan-Rakowski et al., 2023), and issues related to plagiarism (van den Berg & du Plessis, 2023) and academic integrity (Pretorius, 2023). Moreover, researchers also revealed the potential causes leading to educators’ psychological pressure (Wang, 2023) and anxiety towards AI (Sanusi et al., 2024). These causes include educators’ unfamiliarity with AI technologies (Wilton et al., 2022), limited infrastructure about AI (Vazhayil et al., 2019), and lack of comprehensive training materials on data literacy and AI literacy (Yau et al., 2022).
